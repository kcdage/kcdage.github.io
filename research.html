SMC X-1

I am collaborating with Dr. Will Clarkson, searching for a spin-superorbital period relation in SMC X-1. We are using archival RXTE data to search for this link, and have almost concluded our analysis, with a paper in preparation. The AAS poster abstract can be found here .

RZ 2109

RZ 2109 is an ultraluminous black hole in a globular cluster located near NGC 4472. My advisor (Dr. Steve Zepf ) has a number of x-ray datasets, as well as optical. Previous work indicates it is likely to be an ultra compact x-ray binary; the presence of the completely forbidden O[III] transition line in the optical points towards the binary companion being a white dwarf. We hope that long-term optical and x-ray monitoring of this unique object will help shed light on the nature of the source. I presented a poster on this at the 229th AAS meeting in Dallas in January 2017.
