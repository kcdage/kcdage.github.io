Outreach

One of my main focuses in outreach is to make it as hands-on and accessible as possible. Over the spring and summer of 2020, I collaborated with the Abrams Planetarium to live-stream hands-on demonstrations of physics and astronomy experiments. This was aimed at 5-10 year olds affected by the school shut-downs, and the experimental setups consisted only of readily available household items. These were live-streamed through the Planetarium's Facebook account, and reach participants all over the world.

In October 2020, I developed a virtual workshop on astronomy for STEM Santa Fe's 2020 STEM Pathways for Girls conference. Physical kits were delivered to the work-shop attendees, and the event was a mix of background lectures and hands-on experiments about gravity and optics.

Public Engagement

I enjoy giving public lectures at a variety of levels. I've given numerous talks at Astronomy on Tap Lansing and the Capitol Area Astronomy Association in East Lansing, and also planetarium talks for schools and the general public. I'm also on the organizing committee for the virtual Astronomy Trivia Nights hosted by McGill Space Institute.

I have also written exams for the state level Science Olympiad, volunteered at the Abrams Planetarium and the Spartan Young Astronomer's Club, and organized an event for the IAU Women and Girls in Astronomy Month. I also volunteered and organized the astronomy outreach at the MSU Science Festival for several years.
