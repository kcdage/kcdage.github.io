Observing at SOAR

SOAR (SOuthern Astrophysical Research) telescope is a 4.1 meter telescope located on Cerro Pachon, near La Serena, Chile. Cerro Pachon also hosts Gemini South and LSST. There are also a lot of cool birds there, like Sierra Finches and Rufous-banded Miners, and also chilla foxes. You can sometimes see Andean Condors if you're lucky, but I didn't.

The Abrams Planetarium also loaned me a 360 degree camera. You can click to drag the image around to see what the view is like on the mountain. Gemini South is just in the background of the photo below:

This second one shows a closer view of Gemini South:

The third image is a long exposure at night, and you can see the large and small Magellenic clouds, as well as the globular cluster 47 Tuc, the constellation Orion and the Pleiades. SOAR is the nearby dome, and Gemini South is further back.
