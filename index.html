About

I'm currently a postdoctoral fellow at the McGill Space Institute, where I use multiwavelength observations to identify objects in extreme gravitational environments and characterize their physics. I got my PhD in astrophysics in July 2020 from Michigan State University, and my Bachelor of Science in physics at the University of Michigan-Dearborn in 2014.

My work utilizes both optical studies of dense stellar cluster environments, and X-ray studies of objects undergoing accretion in a new regime of physics. These studies probe the population of X-ray binaries in globular clusters, including sources that exhibit extreme accretion physics; and also provide observational evidence of the extent and nature of black holes in the dense clusters of stars outside of our own Galaxy.
